It's been a very low-key week, finished my CAT testing, and had piano lessons!

I came across a very interesting subject (i.e. weird and unnecessary) in the Reading Comprehension part of my CAT test here's one of them I asked a few online friends (not homeschoolers) and they had no idea what it was, like I said, weird. My final score was decent, thogh I do wish I could backtrack and see what I got wrong in the end. On average I got 87% right.

 I'm starting to enjoy Seid Meier's Civilization VI, but it's taken a while, and I still feel intimidated by it sometimes, I found a really good tutorial series by the youtuber quill18, he keeps you attention captivated and is good at explaining all the many details. Be willing to dedicate you time though, there are six parts that are each about 30 min long.

Had a good conversation with a Catholic and ex-Catholic (no longer "religious") it's my first conversation involving religion directly that I've had on Discord, and with a Catholic in general. It started from a very... uh... interesting source. 

This is the only "major" Discord server I participate in (200 members, 20 active) many of them are friends friends, a decent amount of them met Calvin Dunn when he participated in Classical Conversation groups, so the majority of them are christian (to what extent I don't know) It was nice being able to pull up scripture in a conversation, I love how some adults can go right to the Bible on different subjects right away. I hope that I'll be able to do that more and more often as I study and read the Bible.
